Discover Da Nang: The Heart of Vietnam's Central Coast

Located on Vietnam's stunning central coast, Da Nang is a vibrant city known for its perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and modern attractions. From golden beaches to historic temples and scenic mountains, Da Nang offers something for every type of traveler.

Explore Vietnam's Rich Culture and Heritage

Vietnam is a land of extraordinary beauty, where ancient traditions meet modern vibrancy. From the lively streets of Hanoi to the stunning beaches of Da Nang, each city offers a unique adventure.

  • Da Nang Known for its stunning beaches and proximity to the famous ancient town of Hoi An, Da Nang offers a perfect combination of nature and culture. Discover the Marble Mountains, visit the famous Golden Bridge, and unwind on My Khe Beach.
  • Golden Bridge (Cau Vang) One of the most recent additions to Da Nang’s list of must-visit attractions is the Golden Bridge, a pedestrian bridge held up by two giant stone hands. Located in the Ba Na Hills, the bridge offers stunning views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. It has become a symbol of modern Vietnam and is perfect for photos and sightseeing.
  • Stunning Beaches Da Nang is home to some of Vietnam’s most beautiful beaches, offering pristine sand, clear blue waters, and great spots for relaxation and adventure
    My Khe Beach: Often referred to as one of the most beautiful beaches in Vietnam, My Khe Beach boasts fine sand and warm waters perfect for swimming and lounging under the sun.
    Non Nuoc Beach: Located at the foot of the Marble Mountains, Non Nuoc is known for its peaceful atmosphere and is ideal for sunbathing and water sports.
    Bac My An Beach: A quieter alternative, this beach offers great views of the coastline and the nearby Son Tra Peninsula. Whether you're looking to relax on the sand or try some exciting water sports like surfing and jet skiing, Da Nang’s beaches have it all.

Cultural and Historical Sites

Da Nang is rich in history and culture, offering fascinating temples, ancient buildings, and local markets to explore

  • Champa Museum Learn about the ancient Champa civilization that once thrived in Central Vietnam. The museum houses one of the largest collections of Champa sculpture in the world.
  • Linh Ung Pagoda A tranquil spot in the Son Tra Peninsula, this Buddhist pagoda is home to a massive statue of the Lady Buddha, which stands at 67 meters tall. The pagoda offers peaceful surroundings and incredible views over Da Nang.
  • Dragon Bridge An iconic landmark in Da Nang, the Dragon Bridge is an impressive feat of engineering that lights up at night. Every weekend, the dragon breathes fire and water, creating a spectacular show for visitors.
